V. The Core Model

May 16, 2019

What is a core model?

In Ida Aalen's article, The Core Model: Designing Inside Out for Better Results she writes about using a "core model" system to design webpages. The model consists of two key factors, a business objective and fulfilling user tasks. To successfully use this model, you not only fulfill the information reader's hope to find on a site, but convert users to fulfill an objective path of your own.

The main idea of this article was to attract web-developers and convert them to using this storybuilding technique as she says that by using this model it can lead to higher success rates of your own business objectives.

Aalen also talks about the main difference between a core model and wireframe as with wireframing you're only focussing on the design rather than the actual experience going through and the content being displayed to readers.

Finally, Aalen goes to talk about the success rates of using a core model as she uses a lung cancer association as her example, breaking down the flow of their site and how it leads to the end goal of having audiences donate to the association once reading information about the cnacer. She says that by using this format, you will see HUGE success rates as long as you follow this core model and that by focussing on mobile pages there are often higher conversion rates.

Takeaways

From this article I learned a lot about branding and best ways to engage viewers to my content. Wanting to go into either front-end web development or UI/UX, this was a really important article to read as it goes through the idea of how based on the business objective you should emphasize the graphics on the page towards that objective.

However, what audiences may not realise is that she herself is using the core model in this article as she is telling readers to follow this model for better success rates in their own businesses
